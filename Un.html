pOct. 2016 – April 2021,

The United Nation for High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR), Jordan, Amman.

Public Health Associate


Three major-specific responsibilities:

1. Managing Mental Health Psychosocial Support Services (MHPSS) and Community based PSS protection interventions

2. Managing Cash to Access Essential Health Services (CAEHS) project

3. Managing Medical Referral System for refugees Project.

4. Provide support in low-resource and limited-access border areas, specifically

at the Rukban Asylum Seekers Camp

As the lead for Mental Health and Psychosocial Support Services (MHPSS) and Community based PSS protection interventions in the camps, my role involves developing and implementing comprehensive MHPSS & PSS community based Protection programs, coordinating activities, conducting staff training, and collaborating with external stakeholders. I oversee individual and group counseling, psychoeducation programs, and support groups. Furthermore, I monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of interventions and establish partnerships to ensure access to specialized mental health services for refugees. In addition to my MHPSS responsibilities, I manage the medical referral process, coordinate with referral hospitals for payment settlement, and ensure efficient patient service management. As the focal point for the health information system (HIS), I oversee timely submission of reports, assess their quality, and provide on-site supervision. I maintain communication with WHO and MOH regarding the health information system for urban refugees. Furthermore, I manage the Cash for Essential Health Services program and chair the Cash for Health working group in urban areas. I played a pivotal role in promoting and implementing public health programs for asylum seekers at Rukban camp. Responsibilities include coordinating primary, maternity, and child health initiatives, conducting health assessments, monitoring healthcare provision, and supporting disease prevention measures. Collaboration with partners, community leaders, and local authorities ensures effective implementation of interventions. This challenging role requires strong public health knowledge, excellent communication skills, and the ability to address specific health needs in a resource-limited desert environment at Rukban Camp, located at the No-Man's land area between Jordan, Syria, and Iraq.
